Community Building · University of Exeter · 2024–25

Started with 5 people. Grew to 100+ by word of mouth.

During my MSc year at Exeter, the goal was simple: find a few people to play football with. What started as 4–5 individuals quickly grew into a grassroots community of over 100 members across 3 WhatsApp groups — entirely through word of mouth, organic conversations, and showing up at other societies.

The group grew so fast it got difficult to manage, so we segmented — separate groups by interest, preference, and ability. Weekly games for all levels, mini-tournaments with full fixture lists, friendlies against other universities, and socials well beyond the pitch. Players just had to show up.

What we built

01 / Organisation

Games for every level

Casual kick-abouts, competitive matches, and structured mini-tournaments. We bought bibs and footballs. A member even built an AI-powered team-balancing system, rating players to generate fair teams automatically.

02 / Competition

Friendlies & tournaments

Friendlies against other university groups and societies. Mini-tournaments with full fixture lists, results and top scorers tracked to keep things competitive. The structure made people come back week after week.

03 / Community

Beyond the pitch

Relationships built on the pitch became friendships off it — pub crawls, meetups, FIFA nights, pub quizzes, festivals. It became a real community, not just a sports group. That's what made people invite their friends.

The growth story

It started with asking around. 4–5 people became 10, then 20. At some point the original group was overflowing — games oversubscribed, coordination broke down. So we segmented.

"Football Exeter" became the main hub (114 members); "Casuals FC" for the relaxed crowd (83). It wasn't a marketing strategy, it was necessity. But the principles are identical to what I'd do for a brand: understand your audience, remove friction, segment by behaviour, and let the community do the acquisition for you.

Why this matters for marketing

Community building is marketing at its most fundamental. No budget, no ads, no algorithm — just understanding what people want, removing friction, and creating something worth showing up to.

Know your audience, deliver consistent value, segment when it gets unwieldy, make participation effortless, and let the community do the marketing for you. 100+ people joined through nothing but word of mouth — organic growth in its purest form.
