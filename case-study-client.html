Client Work · Content & Production

Two gyms. One city. Everything learned in the gap between them.

A content and production story across two fitness brands in Bangalore — different briefs, different scales, different constraints, and what the work revealed about the distance between making content and building a presence.

01  /  Kintore Fitness Club

Cinematic content for a gym ready to grow.

Mid-tier gym · Vijaya Nagar, Bangalore

The brief

Kintore needed content that matched its ambition. A gym with good equipment and a loyal local base, but a weak social presence. I was brought in with full creative freedom to produce video and photo content fed to an external marketing agency managing their account.

What I did

Pre and post production end to end — concepts, shot lists, on-site direction, cinematic editing in DaVinci Resolve with colour grading and sound design. Captions and carousel copy. During agency transitions I handled stories and posts directly. 40+ assets, all shot, directed, and edited by me.

Video work

On the feed

@kintorefitnessclub

Cinematic reels that pushed the page's organic reach — multiple posts crossed 50K–130K+ views, building a recognisable visual identity the brand hadn't had before.

The content performed — individual reels hit strong view counts organically. But it hit a ceiling. Staff turnover was high, the on-ground experience inconsistent, and what members saw on Instagram didn't always match what they walked into. Content was doing its job; the environment it amplified wasn't.

02  /  SK-27 Gym

A premium brand, built from zero.

Premium gym · RR Nagar, Bangalore

The brief

SK-27 was launching a new branch and needed everything from scratch — Instagram account, content library, brand presence. Full social media management from day one, with no existing audience to build on.

What I did

Managed the account across a concentrated two-month period — launch content, event campaigns, daily engagement. Posts, carousels, reels, daily stories, live sessions, captions. Organised content around marathon partnerships and class launches, and built relationships with members, staff, and local partners on the ground.

Video work

On the feed

@sk27gymbangalore

A full feed built from zero — launch content, class campaigns, spin and community reels driving discovery from a standing start, with posts reaching 70K+ views.

Content amplified something already real. The staff were engaged, the community genuine, and members started scheduling shoots themselves without being asked. The content culture became self-sustaining — because it reflected a place people actually wanted to be part of.

Results

2

100+
Assets shot, directed, and edited end to end.

Across both clients, the work drove visible community engagement and brand awareness — members shared stories and reels organically and engaged through comments, challenges, and UGC. At SK-27, daily story views grew consistently and event campaigns generated real foot traffic. At Kintore, cinematic content gave the brand a visual quality it hadn't had before.

The key insight

The same person. Two gyms. Years of resistance to personal training at Kintore — then a three-month PT commitment signed within weeks at SK-27.

My father was a member at both. The product was identical — strength and conditioning. The difference was trust, community, and the environment the content was operating inside. Content is an amplifier, not a fix. It makes good things louder. It cannot compensate for what isn't there.
